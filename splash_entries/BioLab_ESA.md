# BioLab (ESA Columbus)

**General**  
Operations: CREW TIME FOR LOADING + GROUND TELESCIENCE  
Category: Experiment Hosting Facilities with Centrifuges  
Operator/Vendor: ESA / DLR (MUSC, Cologne)  
Status: Active  
Flight Heritage: Columbus module, ISS  
Year of First Launch: 2008 (7 February, Space Shuttle Atlantis with Columbus)  

**Specifications**  
Mass: Per ESA/Columbus payload documentation  
Size: Rack-mounted in Columbus module; two rotor platforms in incubator  
Payload Volume Required: Columbus rack; experiment containers / vials (ATV, Progress, commercial cargo)  
Power (Average): Per Columbus rack allocation  
Hardware Operational Temperature Range: Controlled incubator (life support in facility)  
Interface Definitions: Experiment containers; standard vials; MELFI storage interface  
Radiation Sensitivity: Standard ISS  
Heat Sync Required: Facility thermal control  

**Research**  
Specimen Types Supported: Micro-organisms, cells, tissue cultures, small plants, small invertebrates  
Specimen Size/Quantity: Standard experiment containers and vials; capacity per rotor platform  
Temperature Range at Sample: Incubator-controlled (facility life support)  
Experiment Duration: 1 day to 3 months  
Centrifuge: Yes (two rotor platforms; 1 g simulated for gravity comparison with weightlessness)  
Imaging: Yes (video cameras on rotor platforms; microscope; teleoperations for ground scientist)  

**Logistics**  
Crew Handling Required: Yes (insert containers, manual loading in BioGlovebox, initiate automatic processing)  
Autonomy: Yes (automatic processing after crew initiation; ground teleoperations)  
Sample Return Required: As per mission (cargo return)  
Compatibility with On-Orbit Sample Processing: Yes (BioGlovebox, Experiment Preparation Unit, MELFI)
